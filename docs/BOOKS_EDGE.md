# Advanced Edge Discovery Reading List (25)

Rigorous, math-heavy sources for discovering, validating, and maintaining edge.

1) Advances in Financial Machine Learning — Marcos Lopez de Prado
2) Machine Learning for Asset Managers — Marcos Lopez de Prado
3) Active Portfolio Management — Richard C. Grinold, Ronald N. Kahn
4) Quantitative Trading — Ernest P. Chan
5) Algorithmic Trading: Winning Strategies and Their Rationale — Ernest P. Chan
6) Algorithmic Trading and DMA — Barry Johnson
7) Trading and Exchanges — Larry Harris
8) Market Microstructure Theory — Maureen O'Hara
9) Empirical Market Microstructure — Joel Hasbrouck
10) High-Frequency Trading — Irene Aldridge
11) Statistics and Data Analysis for Financial Engineering — David Ruppert
12) The Econometrics of Financial Markets — Campbell, Lo, MacKinlay
13) Time Series Analysis — James D. Hamilton
14) Stochastic Calculus for Finance I — Steven E. Shreve
15) Stochastic Calculus for Finance II — Steven E. Shreve
16) Arbitrage Theory in Continuous Time — Tomas Bjork
17) Asset Pricing — John H. Cochrane
18) Risk and Asset Allocation — Attilio Meucci
19) Options, Futures, and Other Derivatives — John C. Hull
20) The Elements of Statistical Learning — Hastie, Tibshirani, Friedman
21) Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning — Christopher M. Bishop
22) Machine Learning: A Probabilistic Perspective — Kevin P. Murphy
23) Statistical Arbitrage: Algorithmic Trading Insights and Techniques — Andrew Pole
24) Financial Modeling — Simon Benninga
25) Expected Returns — Antti Ilmanen

Edge concepts these sources emphasize:
- Information ratio, alpha decay, and the fundamental law of active management
- Market microstructure frictions and execution costs
- Regime shifts and non-stationarity
- Leakage-safe validation (purged CV, embargo, CPCV)
- Model uncertainty and risk-aware sizing
- Transaction costs and capacity constraints
